The Robot Social Navigation Problem

  • Robots are increasingly entering human-populated spaces (hospitals, malls, airports)
  • Key Question: How can a robot move through a busy crowd safely and politely?
  • Current robots often struggle to avoid people smoothly or follow social norms

Context – Why Social Navigation Matters

  • High Impact: In healthcare, hospitality, etc., robots must navigate around people to be truly useful
  • Socially Aware Navigation: Beyond just avoiding collisions, robots should respect personal space and social norms
  • Integration into Society: Effective social navigation is critical for robots to be accepted in daily life

Challenges in Human–Robot Coexistence

  • Unpredictable Humans: People can stop, turn, or crowd unexpectedly – hard to model this behavior
  • Dynamic Environments: Crowds create a constantly changing maze; static path-planning isn’t enough
  • Safety vs. Efficiency: Robots must avoid accidents while reaching goals quickly – a tough balance
  • Social Norms: Behaviors like keeping distance or queueing are implicit – robots must learn them

Research Questions

RQ1: How can predictive world models improve decision-making in social robot navigation?

  • Investigates whether anticipating human movements leads to better robot path planning

RQ2: What challenges arise when using Reinforcement Learning in continuous robot control for social navigation, and how can we address them?

  • Examines difficulties transitioning from discrete to smooth motion controls and how to overcome them

These questions set the stage for developing a new approach to socially aware robot navigation.
