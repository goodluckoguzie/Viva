Cosine-Gated LSTM (CGLSTM)

ğŸ“Œ Introduction

  • Proposes a novel LSTM-based architecture with a cosine similarity gate.
  • Aims to improve sequence prediction by focusing on directional alignment.
  • Builds on limitations observed in previous LSTM experiments (e.g., in SocNavGym).

ğŸ” Fundamentals & Limitations

  • LSTM overcomes vanishing gradients but struggles with long-term dependencies.
  • Sensitive to outliers and may not gate optimally in dynamic environments.
  • Motivates enhancing the gating mechanism with cosine similarity.

ğŸ’¡ Why Cosine-Gated LSTM?

  • Cosine similarity emphasizes vector orientation over magnitude.
  • Helps the model focus on the most relevant sequence features.
  • Leads to more robust gating and improved long-term dependency modeling.

ğŸ—ï¸ CGLSTM Architecture

ğŸŒ€ Cosine Similarity Gate

  • Gate-ic: Compares transformed input vs. previous output alignment.
  • Gate-co: Measures alignment with the current output state.
  • Combined gating filters out noise and emphasizes relevant features.

ğŸ§ª Datasets & Evaluation

  • FallingBallEnv: Tests fundamental motion prediction (balanced & unbalanced).
  • Benchmark tasks: Adding Problem, row-wise MNIST, Fashion-MNIST, IMDB, PTB.
  • SocNavGym: Real-world-like social navigation scenario.
  • Metrics: MAE/MSE, accuracy, perplexity, training/testing time, t-tests.

Adaptive Predictive Reinforcement Learning: Entropy-Driven Adaptive Prediction Horizons

ğŸ“Œ Introduction

  • Addresses limitations of fixed prediction horizons in continuous RL environments.
  • Integrates entropy-driven adaptive horizons with advanced predictive modeling.
  • Builds on our previous work in predictive world models for social navigation.

ğŸ’¡ Motivation

  • Fixed horizons can waste computation in simple tasks and underperform in complex ones.
  • Continuous environments exhibit variable uncertainty, requiring adaptable planning.
  • Policy entropy provides a natural signal for dynamically adjusting prediction depth.

ğŸ¯ Research Objectives & Contributions

  • Develop an entropy-driven adaptive horizon mechanism within SAC.
  • Integrate CGLSTM into DreamerV3 for enhanced predictive modeling.
  • Benchmark against state-of-the-art RL algorithms (SAC, PPO, DDPG, TD3, DreamerV3 variants).

ğŸ† Significance

  • Adaptive horizons adjust in real time to environmental uncertainty.
  • Enhances long-term planning and decision-making in continuous action spaces.
  • Improves computational efficiency by avoiding unnecessary predictions.
